People: can’t live with them, can’t ghost them forever. Whether it’s decoding your friend's passive-aggressive emoji or surviving small talk with your neighbor who treats his cat like a child — we’ve got the guide.

Step 1: Learn to Nod (It’s a Superpower)

You don’t always need to respond with words. Master the thoughtful nod — it works in meetings, awkward family dinners, and when someone starts telling you their dream from last night.

Step 2: Group Chats Are Traps

Never say anything in a group chat you wouldn't say on a billboard. Group chats are eternal, and someone always screenshots. Stay safe.

Step 3: The Compliment Sandwich

Want to give feedback without sounding rude? Sandwich it. Compliment, suggestion, compliment. Example: “You're so good at showing up late — it's kind of impressive.”

Step 4: Boundaries Are Cool

Saying "no" is not rude. It's a sentence. Use it. You’re not a Netflix subscription — you don’t have to be available all the time.

Step 5: Fun Facts About People

  • 80% of small talk is about weather. The rest is about pretending to like salad.
  • Sarcasm is the sixth love language.
  • People remember how you made them feel... especially if it was awkward.
  • If you wave back at someone who wasn't waving at you, you legally gain +2 charisma points.

Final Thoughts

People are weird, you’re weird — it works out. Embrace the chaos with empathy, humor, and maybe noise-cancelling headphones. You got this.
