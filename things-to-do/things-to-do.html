Life’s short and your to-do list is empty? Let’s fix that with some wonderfully weird, joyfully productive, and questionably useful activities.

Step 1: Invent a Holiday

Today is International Compliment-Your-Plants Day. Tell your fern it’s thriving and your cactus it’s looking sharp.

Step 2: Reorganize Something You Don’t Own

Mentally rearrange your friend’s bookshelf. Out loud. With confidence.

Step 3: Write a Letter to Future You

“Dear future me, do we still dance while brushing our teeth?” Important check-ins only.

Step 4: DIY Something… Anything

A pencil holder made of spoons? A candle stand from old DVDs? You’re not just creative—you’re unstoppable.

Step 5: Make a To-Don't List

Things you absolutely refuse to do today: respond to emails, fold laundry, and act normal.

Step 6: Pretend You’re a Food Critic

Review your lunch with big words. “This instant noodle experience evokes a sense of culinary nostalgia.”

Fun Facts:

  • Honey never spoils. So technically, you could add it to every snack forever.
  • Cows have best friends. Try being that for someone today.
  • You burn more calories frowning than smiling. (But you look cooler smiling.)
